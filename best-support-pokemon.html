Best Support Pokemon in Pokemon Champions — The Backbone of Every VGC Team

Apr 8, 2026By InciniForge9 min read

In VGC doubles, your support Pokemon are the difference between winning and losing. They set up speed control, disrupt your opponent's game plan, protect your sweepers, and enable combos that wouldn't be possible in singles. With the 200+ Pokemon roster now live, including new additions like Florges, Passimian, and Bellibolt, there are more support options than ever. Here are the 8 best support Pokemon in Pokemon Champions and exactly how to build each one.

What Makes a Great Support Pokemon?

The best supports in VGC doubles provide at least two of these:

  • Speed control — Tailwind, Trick Room, Icy Wind, Thunder Wave
  • Disruption — Fake Out, Intimidate, Taunt, Encore
  • Protection — Follow Me, Rage Powder, screens (Reflect/Light Screen)
  • Pivoting — Parting Shot, U-turn, Volt Switch

Incineroar S TIER

Role: Intimidate + Fake Out + Pivot · Usage: 62.4% · Type: Fire/Dark

Why It's #1

The undisputed king of VGC support. Incineroar provides three layers of disruption every time it enters the field: Intimidate drops both opponents' Attack, Fake Out flinches one target for a free turn, and Parting Shot drops stats while pivoting out to cycle Intimidate again. No other Pokemon does all three.

The Build

Nature: Impish (+Def / -SpA) · Item: Assault Vest (34.6%) or Safety Goggles (20.9%)
EVs: 244 HP / 188 Def / 76 SpD
Moves: Fake Out (99.5%) · Knock Off (92.3%) · Flare Blitz (68.7%) · Parting Shot (47.8%)

When to Use It

Every team. Seriously. There's a reason it's on 62% of teams. If you're not running Incineroar, you need a very good reason why. Read the full Incineroar guide →

Whimsicott A TIER

Role: Prankster Tailwind · Usage: 22.1% · Type: Grass/Fairy

Why It's Elite

Prankster gives all status moves +1 priority, meaning Whimsicott's Tailwind goes before anything except Fake Out. This guarantees your team gets doubled Speed for 4 turns. Beyond Tailwind, it can Taunt Trick Room setters, Encore locked-in opponents, or Helping Hand for a huge damage boost.

The Build

Nature: Timid (+Spe / -Atk) · Item: Focus Sash
EVs: 252 HP / 4 SpD / 252 Spe
Moves: Tailwind · Taunt · Encore / Helping Hand · Moonblast / Protect

When to Use It

Any team that wants guaranteed Tailwind. Especially effective alongside fast sweepers like Garchomp, Dragapult, and Greninja who become nearly unstoppable under Tailwind.

Grimmsnarl A TIER

Role: Prankster Screens + Thunder Wave · Usage: 12.8% · Type: Dark/Fairy

Why It's Elite

Grimmsnarl's Prankster turns Reflect and Light Screen into priority moves, halving damage for your entire team for 5 turns. Prankster Thunder Wave cripples fast sweepers permanently (0.25x Speed). Dark typing makes it immune to opposing Prankster users (their priority status moves don't work on Dark types).

The Build

Nature: Careful (+SpD / -SpA) · Item: Light Clay (extends screens to 8 turns)
EVs: 252 HP / 4 Def / 252 SpD
Moves: Reflect · Light Screen · Thunder Wave · Spirit Break / Foul Play

When to Use It

Teams with frail sweepers that need damage reduction to survive (like Kingambit or Garchomp). Also great on Sand teams where Tyranitar and Excadrill appreciate the extra bulk from screens.

Hatterene TRICK ROOM

Role: Trick Room setter + Magic Bounce · Type: Psychic/Fairy

Why It's Elite

Hatterene is the premier Trick Room setter in Champions. Magic Bounce reflects status moves (Taunt, Thunder Wave, Spore) back at the user — meaning opponents can't stop your Trick Room with Taunt. Base 29 Speed means it moves first under Trick Room. 136 SpA gives it real offensive threat too.

The Build

Nature: Quiet (+SpA / -Spe) for minimum Speed · Item: Focus Sash or Mental Herb
EVs: 252 HP / 252 SpA / 4 SpD
Moves: Trick Room · Dazzling Gleam · Psychic / Psyshock · Protect

When to Use It

Dedicated Trick Room teams. Pairs perfectly with slow powerhouses like Torkoal (Eruption under TR), Kingambit, and Snorlax.

Farigiraf TRICK ROOM

Role: Armor Tail + Trick Room · Type: Normal/Psychic

Why It's Elite

Armor Tail blocks ALL priority moves from opponents — Fake Out, Sucker Punch, Extreme Speed, Prankster Taunt, everything. This is insane for Trick Room teams because the biggest threat to setting TR is getting Fake Out'd. With Farigiraf on the field, the opponent simply cannot use priority.

The Build

Nature: Sassy (+SpD / -Spe) for minimum Speed · Item: Sitrus Berry or Mental Herb
EVs: 252 HP / 4 Def / 252 SpD
Moves: Trick Room · Helping Hand · Hyper Voice · Protect

When to Use It

Trick Room teams that need to guarantee TR goes up. Armor Tail + Trick Room is the most reliable TR setup in the game. Normal/Psychic typing gives Ghost immunity (Normal) while hitting Fighting-types (Psychic).

Tsareena ANTI-PRIORITY

Role: Queenly Majesty + Physical Attacker · Type: Grass

Why It's Elite

Queenly Majesty blocks priority moves against your entire side — the same effect as Armor Tail but on an offensive Pokemon. Unlike Farigiraf, Tsareena has 120 Attack and access to Power Whip, High Jump Kick, and U-turn. It's a support and attacker in one slot.

The Build

Nature: Jolly (+Spe / -SpA) · Item: Assault Vest or Focus Sash
EVs: 4 HP / 252 Atk / 252 Spe
Moves: Power Whip · High Jump Kick · U-turn · Protect / Triple Axel

When to Use It

Teams that need priority blocking but also want offensive presence. Great alongside Dragapult and Garchomp who hate Sucker Punch and Ice Shard.

Azumarill REDIRECTION

Role: Follow Me + Huge Power Attacker · Type: Water/Fairy

Why It's Elite

Follow Me redirects all single-target moves to Azumarill, protecting your partner from being targeted. Combined with Huge Power (doubles Attack), Azumarill is one of the rare support Pokemon that can also threaten KOs. Aqua Jet gives it water-type priority. Water/Fairy is an excellent defensive typing with only 3 weaknesses.

The Build

Nature: Adamant (+Atk / -SpA) · Item: Sitrus Berry
EVs: 252 HP / 252 Atk / 4 SpD
Moves: Follow Me · Aqua Jet · Play Rough · Protect

When to Use It

Teams with setup sweepers or Trick Room setters that need guaranteed protection on the setup turn. Also excellent alongside Mega Metagross (redirects Fire/Ground attacks) or Mega Charizard Y (redirects Rock moves).

Milotic ANTI-INTIMIDATE

Role: Competitive + Bulky Water · Type: Water

Why It's Elite

Competitive raises Special Attack by +2 whenever any stat is lowered. In a meta where 62% of teams have Intimidate Incineroar, Milotic gets a free +2 SpA boost every time Incineroar switches in. This turns an opponent's support into your win condition.

The Build

Nature: Modest (+SpA / -Atk) or Bold (+Def / -Atk) · Item: Leftovers or Sitrus Berry
EVs: 252 HP / 252 SpA / 4 SpD
Moves: Scald · Ice Beam · Protect · Coil / Recover

When to Use It

Any team that expects to face lots of Incineroar (which is every team). Milotic forces opponents to stop cycling Intimidate or give you free SpA boosts. Also a reliable Scald user — 30% burn chance cripples physical attackers.

Support Pokemon Tier Summary

  • S Tier: Incineroar — run it on every team
  • A Tier: Whimsicott (Tailwind), Grimmsnarl (Screens)
  • Trick Room: Hatterene (Magic Bounce), Farigiraf (Armor Tail)
  • Anti-Priority: Tsareena (offensive), Farigiraf (defensive)
  • Redirection: Azumarill (Follow Me + Huge Power)
  • Anti-Intimidate: Milotic (Competitive)
