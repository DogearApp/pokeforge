Pokemon Champions Mega Evolution Tier List 2026 — Best Megas Ranked

Updated: Apr 8, 2026By InciniForge7 min read

Mega Evolutions are back in Pokemon Champions, and they're a game-changer for competitive VGC doubles. With both classic megas and new Legends Z-A additions, choosing the right Mega Evolution is one of the most important team building decisions. Here's every competitively viable mega ranked.

Remember: you can only Mega Evolve one Pokemon per team, and using Mega Evolution means you cannot Terastallize in that battle (one gimmick per battle). Choose wisely.

S Tier — Build Your Team Around These

S TIER

Mega Metagross

Win Rate: 50.1% · Usage: 16.7% · Type: Steel/Psychic

Why it's S Tier: Mega Metagross gains Tough Claws, boosting contact moves by 30%. Combined with its massive 145 Attack and 110 Speed, it hits like a truck with Iron Head, Zen Headbutt, and Ice Punch. Steel/Psychic typing gives excellent defensive coverage. Clear Body pre-mega prevents Intimidate drops on the turn you Mega Evolve.

Best Set: Jolly, 252 Atk / 252 Spe / 4 HP. Moves: Iron Head, Zen Headbutt, Ice Punch, Protect. Item: Metagrossite.

S TIER

Mega Charizard Y

Win Rate: 53.1% · Type: Fire/Flying

Why it's S Tier: The highest win rate mega in the game. Mega Charizard Y's Drought sets Sun automatically, boosting its own Fire STAB while weakening Water moves. With 159 Special Attack, Sun-boosted Heat Wave in doubles is devastating spread damage. It also enables Chlorophyll partners like Venusaur.

Best Set: Timid, 252 SpA / 252 Spe / 4 HP. Moves: Heat Wave, Solar Beam, Overheat, Protect. Item: Charizardite Y.

S TIER

Mega Kangaskhan

Win Rate: 49.1% · Usage: 14.2% · Type: Normal

Why it's S Tier: Parental Bond hits twice with every move (second hit at 25% power), effectively giving a 25% damage boost to everything. Fake Out + Parental Bond does massive chip damage turn 1. Power-Up Punch raises Attack twice per use. Normal typing means only one weakness (Fighting) and Ghost immunity.

Best Set: Jolly, 252 Atk / 252 Spe / 4 HP. Moves: Fake Out, Return, Power-Up Punch/Sucker Punch, Protect. Item: Kangaskhanite.

A Tier — Excellent Choices

A TIER

Mega Garchomp

Win Rate: 51.6% · Type: Dragon/Ground

Why it's A Tier: Mega Garchomp trades some Speed for enormous Attack (170) and Special Attack (120). Sand Force boosts Ground, Rock, and Steel moves by 30% in Sandstorm. Pairs perfectly with Tyranitar's Sand Stream. Earthquake under Sand Force is one of the hardest-hitting spread moves in the game.

Best Set: Adamant, 252 Atk / 252 Spe / 4 HP. Moves: Earthquake, Dragon Claw, Rock Slide, Protect. Item: Garchompite.

A TIER

Mega Pinsir

Win Rate: 54.8% · Type: Bug/Flying

Why it's A Tier: The highest raw win rate of any mega. Aerilate turns Normal moves into Flying-type with a 20% power boost. Return becomes a 156 base power Flying STAB. Quick Attack becomes priority Flying STAB. Incredible sweeping potential but requires support to get going.

Best Set: Jolly, 252 Atk / 252 Spe / 4 HP. Moves: Return, Quick Attack, Close Combat, Protect. Item: Pinsirite.

A TIER

Mega Salamence

Type: Dragon/Flying

Why it's A Tier: Also gets Aerilate, turning Hyper Voice into a 117 BP spread Flying STAB that hits through Substitutes. Intimidate before Mega Evolving is a massive bonus. 120/130 offensive stats and 120 Defense make it bulkier than you'd expect.

Best Set: Timid, 252 SpA / 252 Spe / 4 HP. Moves: Hyper Voice, Draco Meteor, Flamethrower, Protect. Item: Salamencite.

A TIER

Mega Gardevoir

Win Rate: 49.7% · Type: Psychic/Fairy

Why it's A Tier: Pixilate turns Hyper Voice into a 117 BP Fairy-type spread move. Psychic/Fairy is an excellent offensive typing. 165 Special Attack is enormous. Hyper Voice pierces Substitutes and hits both opponents. Great into Dragon-heavy and Fighting-heavy teams.

Best Set: Timid, 252 SpA / 252 Spe / 4 HP. Moves: Hyper Voice, Psyshock, Shadow Ball, Protect. Item: Gardevoirite.

B Tier — Solid in the Right Team

B TIER

Mega Tyranitar · Mega Venusaur · Mega Lucario · Mega Scizor · Mega Gyarados

Mega Tyranitar gets massive bulk and keeps Sand Stream — 164 base Attack with great coverage. Mega Venusaur gains Thick Fat, removing its Fire and Ice weaknesses — pairs perfectly with Torkoal Sun. Mega Lucario has Adaptability for 2x STAB on its Fighting/Steel moves. Mega Scizor gets Technician + 150 Attack for devastating Bullet Punches. Mega Gyarados becomes Water/Dark with Mold Breaker.

New Z-A Megas Worth Watching

NEW

Mega Froslass · Mega Dragonite · Mega Greninja · Mega Kingambit

The Legends Z-A megas are still being explored. Mega Froslass gains Snow Warning for automatic snow. Mega Dragonite keeps Multiscale. Mega Greninja is expected to be extremely fast with boosted offenses. Mega Kingambit could push an already S-tier Pokemon even higher. Watch these closely as the meta develops.

How to Choose Your Mega

  • Mega Metagross if you want the most consistent, versatile mega
  • Mega Charizard Y if you're building a Sun team
  • Mega Kangaskhan if you want raw pressure with Fake Out + Parental Bond
  • Mega Garchomp if you're running Sand
  • Mega Gardevoir/Salamence if you want spread Hyper Voice damage
