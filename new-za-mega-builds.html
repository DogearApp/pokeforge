New Legends Z-A Mega Evolutions — The Most Exciting Builds for Pokemon Champions

Apr 4, 2026By InciniForge9 min read

Pokemon Champions brings 40+ new Mega Evolutions from Legends Z-A alongside the classics. These new megas have brand-new abilities that are already shaking up competitive theory. Here are the four generating the most buzz — and how to build around them.

Mega Feraligatr NEW ABILITY

Type: Water · Ability: Dragonize · Champions Lab WR: 53.2%

What Is Dragonize?

Dragonize turns all Normal-type moves into Dragon-type moves and boosts their power by 20%. This is similar to Mega Salamence's Aerilate or Mega Gardevoir's Pixilate — but for Dragon-type. The implications are massive:

  • Return becomes a 156 BP Dragon STAB move
  • Double-Edge becomes a 144 BP Dragon nuke
  • Hyper Beam becomes a 180 BP Dragon attack (with recharge)
  • Quick Attack becomes Dragon-type priority
  • Fake Out becomes a Dragon-type flinch move

Combined with Water STAB, Mega Feraligatr has incredible dual coverage. Dragon + Water is only resisted by Fairy-types and Empoleon.

The Build

Nature: Adamant (+Atk / -SpA) or Jolly (+Spe / -SpA)
EVs: 4 HP / 252 Atk / 252 Spe
Item: Feraligite
Moves:
  • Return — 156 BP Dragon STAB via Dragonize. Main nuke.
  • Liquidation — Water STAB for coverage. Hits Fire/Ground/Rock.
  • Ice Punch — Covers opposing Dragon-types and Grass-types.
  • Protect — Standard VGC staple.

Alternative: Dragon Dance over Protect for a setup sweeper variant. One DD makes Mega Feraligatr nearly unstoppable.

Best Partners

  • Politoed / Pelipper — Rain boosts Water moves while Dragonize handles everything else
  • Incineroar — Fake Out + Intimidate protects Feraligatr's setup turn
  • Fairy-type counter (Metagross, Excadrill) — Fairies resist Dragon, so bring Steel coverage

Mega Meganium NEW ABILITY

Type: Grass/Fairy · Ability: Mega Sol

What Is Mega Sol?

Mega Sol makes all of Meganium's moves act as if the weather is harsh sunlight — without actually setting Sun on the field. This means:

  • Fire-type coverage moves get a 50% boost (without your opponents benefiting)
  • Solar Beam has no charge turn (just for Meganium)
  • Protosynthesis on your allies does NOT activate (it's personal sun, not field sun)
  • Your opponent's Rain team still works — Mega Sol doesn't change field weather

This is a completely unique ability. Meganium gets the benefits of Sun without the drawbacks of a weather war.

The Build

Nature: Modest (+SpA / -Atk) or Bold (+Def / -Atk)
EVs: 252 HP / 252 SpA / 4 SpD
Item: Meganiumite
Moves:
  • Giga Drain — Grass STAB + recovery. Consistent damage.
  • Solar Beam — No charge needed thanks to Mega Sol. 120 BP Grass nuke.
  • Ancient Power / Weather Ball — Weather Ball becomes 100 BP Fire-type under Mega Sol. Ancient Power for coverage.
  • Protect — VGC essential.

Best Partners

  • Grass/Fairy typing is excellent defensively — resists Water, Electric, Ground, Fighting, Dark
  • Pairs well with Pokemon that appreciate NOT having Sun on the field (Rain abusers can coexist)
  • Kingambit — Covers Meganium's Poison and Steel weaknesses

Mega Scovillain NEW ABILITY

Type: Grass/Fire · Ability: Spicy Spray

What Is Spicy Spray?

Any opponent that attacks Mega Scovillain gets burned. Any move, any type — physical or special. Contact or ranged. The Smogon community has called this ability "uniquely broken" and said "we're LUCKY this is on Scovillain's stats."

This means:

  • Physical attackers take 50% Attack reduction from burn AND residual damage
  • U-turn users get burned before switching out
  • Rapid Spin and Knock Off users get punished
  • Even special attackers take burn chip damage (1/16 HP per turn)

The Build

Nature: Calm (+SpD / -Atk) or Bold (+Def / -Atk)
EVs: 252 HP / 252 Def / 4 SpD or 252 HP / 128 Def / 128 SpD
Item: Scovillainite
Moves:
  • Leech Seed — Recovery + chip damage. Forces the opponent to attack you (triggering burn).
  • Protect — Stall for Leech Seed + burn damage. Essential.
  • Flamethrower — Fire STAB for damage when needed.
  • Giga Drain — Grass STAB + recovery.

Alternative: Rest + Sleep Talk for maximum stall. You burn everything that attacks you, then heal off the damage.

How It Fits

Mega Scovillain isn't a sweeper — it's a disruptive wall. Bring it in against physical-heavy teams and watch them crumble as every attacker gets burned. Best on stall or balance teams. 65/85/85 defenses are mediocre, so it needs redirection (Follow Me) or screens (Reflect) support to survive long enough to matter.

Mega Froslass NEW MEGA

Type: Ice/Ghost · Ability: Snow Warning · Champions Lab WR: 58.3%

Why It's Exciting

Mega Froslass has the third-highest win rate of any mega archetype on Champions Lab (58.3%). Snow Warning sets Snow on Mega Evolution, boosting Ice-type Defense by 50%. Combined with Ghost typing (3 immunities: Normal, Fighting, Ground), Mega Froslass is surprisingly bulky and hard to take down.

The Build

Nature: Timid (+Spe / -Atk)
EVs: 4 HP / 252 SpA / 252 Spe
Item: Froslassite
Moves:
  • Blizzard — Never misses in Snow. 110 BP spread Ice STAB.
  • Shadow Ball — Ghost STAB. Great coverage.
  • Icy Wind — Speed control by lowering both opponents' Speed.
  • Protect

Best Partners

  • Slush Rush users (Alolan Ninetales is not in Champions, but Beartic/Excadrill in snow can work)
  • Alolan Ninetales — Wait, not in Champions. Use Froslass AS the Snow setter.
  • Garchomp — Immune to the Electric moves that threaten... actually Ice/Ghost has different weaknesses. Fire, Dark, Rock, Steel, Ghost.
  • Incineroar — Handles Steel and Dark types that threaten Froslass.

Which New Mega Should You Try First?

  • Mega Feraligatr if you want a unique Dragon sweeper with insane coverage
  • Mega Meganium if you want Sun benefits without the weather war
  • Mega Scovillain if you love disruptive stall and making opponents miserable
  • Mega Froslass if you want the highest win rate new mega with Snow synergy
