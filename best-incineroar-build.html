Best Incineroar Build for Pokemon Champions 2026 — The Complete Guide

Updated: April 4, 2026By InciniForge6 min read

Incineroar is the most used Pokemon in Pokemon Champions with a staggering 62.4% usage rate. If you're playing competitive VGC doubles, you will face Incineroar in almost every match. Here's everything you need to know to use it — or beat it.

Why Incineroar Is #1

Incineroar does three things better than any other Pokemon in the game:

  • Intimidate — Lowers both opponents' Attack by one stage on switch-in. In doubles, this effectively reduces physical damage by 33% for your entire side.
  • Fake Out — Priority flinch move that stops one opponent from acting on turn 1. Guarantees your partner can set up Tailwind, Trick Room, or attack freely.
  • Parting Shot — Lowers the opponent's Attack AND Special Attack by one stage, then switches Incineroar out. You can cycle Intimidate again next time it comes in.

This combination of disruption, support, and pivoting makes Incineroar the best support Pokemon in VGC history.

Move-by-Move Breakdown

Fake Out (99.5% usage)

The defining move. Priority +3, guaranteed flinch on the first turn Incineroar is out. Use it to stop an opponent from attacking while your partner sets up. You can cycle Fake Out by switching out and back in.

Knock Off (92.3% usage)

Removes the opponent's held item AND deals good Dark STAB damage. Hitting Eviolite users, Berry holders, or Choice item users with Knock Off cripples them for the rest of the match. 97.5 BP when the opponent has an item.

Flare Blitz (68.7% usage)

Fire STAB with 120 base power. Hits Steel-types like Metagross, Scizor, and Ferrothorn hard. Recoil damage hurts, but Incineroar often doesn't need to last — it just needs to disrupt and pivot.

Parting Shot (47.8% usage)

The key to cycling Intimidate. Lowers the opponent's Attack and Special Attack, then switches Incineroar out. Bring it back later for another Intimidate + Fake Out. Some players run U-turn (45.2%) instead for damage while pivoting.

Item Choice

  • Assault Vest (34.6%) — Boosts Special Defense by 50%. Makes Incineroar incredibly bulky on both sides. Can't use Protect or status moves, so no Parting Shot — run U-turn instead.
  • Rocky Helmet (29%) — Punishes physical attackers that make contact. Great against Fake Out users and U-turn.
  • Safety Goggles (20.9%) — Immune to Spore from Amoonguss and powder moves. Also ignores Sandstorm/Hail chip.
  • Sitrus Berry (7.8%) — Simple HP recovery. Lets Incineroar take a few more hits while cycling in and out.

Tera Type Choice

  • Tera Water (35%) — Removes Water, Ground, and Rock weaknesses. Turns Incineroar into a bulky Water-type that resists the common attacking types.
  • Tera Bug (34%) — Unique defensive profile. Resists Ground and Fighting (two of Incineroar's biggest threats).
  • Tera Grass (16.5%) — Covers Water and Ground weaknesses. Useful against Rain teams.
  • Tera Ghost (12.8%) — Immune to Fake Out and Fighting moves. Makes opposing Incineroar's Fake Out useless.

Best Team Partners

Incineroar fits on virtually every team, but these partners benefit the most:

  • Garchomp — Intimidate softens physical hits for Garchomp. Incineroar resists Fairy and Ice (Garchomp's weaknesses).
  • Dragapult — Fake Out protects Dragapult while it attacks freely. Ghost-type immunity lets it switch into predicted Fighting moves.
  • Kingambit — Defiant raises Attack when Intimidated by opposing Incineroar. Incineroar's Fake Out protects Kingambit's setup turns.
  • Whimsicott — Fake Out + Tailwind is the most consistent turn 1 in the game.
  • Any Trick Room setter — Fake Out the opponent threatening your TR setter. Guaranteed Trick Room.

How to Beat Incineroar

Since you'll face it every match, know the counterplay:

  • Defiant / Competitive — Kingambit and Milotic punish Intimidate with stat boosts.
  • Inner Focus / Clear Body — Immune to Intimidate. Metagross and Dragapult are common examples.
  • Rock-type moves — Incineroar is 4x weak to Rock after Tera if it doesn't Tera. Stone Edge and Rock Slide do massive damage.
  • Taunt — Stops Parting Shot and Fake Out (after turn 1).
  • Ground-type moves — Earthquake hits Incineroar hard, especially with Life Orb or STAB.
