Pokemon Champions Tier List 2026 — Every Pokemon Ranked for VGC Doubles

Updated: Apr 8, 2026By InciniForge8 min read

This is the definitive Pokemon Champions tier list for the live meta. We've ranked every competitively viable Pokemon in the 200+ Champions roster based on usage rates, win rates, versatility, and team impact from tournament data and competitive battles. Notable new additions like Machamp, Luxray, Toxicroak, Reuniclus, Florges, and Bellibolt are still finding their place in the meta.

S Tier — Metagame Defining

Run on almost every team. Centralizing presence in the meta.

Incineroar 62.4% usage Kingambit 35.7% Dragapult 28.3% Garchomp 24.1%

Incineroar is the undisputed king of Champions VGC. Intimidate + Fake Out + Parting Shot makes it the best support Pokemon in the game. Kingambit thrives with Supreme Overlord, getting stronger as teammates fall — a terrifying late-game cleaner. Dragapult has unmatched Speed and Ghost/Dragon coverage. Garchomp brings Earthquake dominance and Mega Evolution potential.

A Tier — Top Picks

Excellent Pokemon that fit on many teams and define archetypes.

Whimsicott 22.1% Tyranitar 19.6% Aegislash 18.9% Lucario 18.2% Metagross 16.7% Greninja 15.6% Torkoal 14.8% Excadrill 13.2% Grimmsnarl 12.8% Hatterene 11.4%

Whimsicott is the premier Prankster Tailwind setter. Tyranitar anchors Sand teams and Mega Evolves into a monster. Metagross has one of the best Mega Evolutions with Clear Body. Excadrill doubles Speed in Sand. Grimmsnarl provides Prankster screens.

B Tier — Solid Picks

Strong Pokemon that excel in specific team compositions.

Venusaur Charizard Politoed Pelipper Milotic Arcanine Kommo-o Corviknight Noivern Talonflame Weavile Hydreigon Dragonite Scizor Volcarona Meowscarada Skeledirge Garganacl

Venusaur is essential for Sun teams with Chlorophyll. Charizard has a devastating Mega Y form. Politoed/Pelipper enable Rain cores. Corviknight provides amazing Steel/Flying defensive typing. Dragonite has Multiscale and Extreme Speed.

C Tier — Niche Picks

Useful in specific matchups or compositions but not universally strong.

Froslass Chandelure Toxapex Tsareena Mimikyu Primarina Azumarill Snorlax Goodra Tinkaton Armarouge Ceruledge Quaquaval Dondozo Farigiraf

Froslass has a new Mega Evolution from Legends Z-A. Tsareena blocks priority with Queenly Majesty. Farigiraf blocks priority with Armor Tail and sets Trick Room. Dondozo pairs with Tatsugiri for the Commander combo.

D Tier — Situational

Rarely seen but can work in the right hands or specific matchups.

Sableye Oranguru Mudsdale Vanilluxe Crabominable Drampa Klefki Furfrou Gourgeist Toucannon

These Pokemon can surprise opponents but generally have better alternatives. Sableye has Prankster but lacks offensive presence. Oranguru can set Trick Room but is outclassed by Hatterene and Farigiraf.

How We Ranked These Pokemon

Rankings are based on a combination of:

  • Usage rate from early Champions tournament data and simulated battles
  • Win rate across thousands of competitive matches
  • Versatility — how many team archetypes the Pokemon fits on
  • Mega Evolution potential — Pokemon with strong megas get a boost
  • Team impact — does this Pokemon make every team it's on better?

This tier list will be updated as the meta evolves. Check back regularly for changes.
